Vinton Cerf

Vinton Cerf is widely known as the "Father of the Internet".Vint Cerf (born on 1943) is an American computer scientist. He earned his Ph.D. from UCLA in 1972. At UCLA he worked in Professor Leonard Kleinrock's networking group that connected the first two nodes of the ARPANET and contributed to the ARPANET host-to-host protocol. At Standford University, he conducted research on packet network interconnection protocols and co-designed the DoD TCP/IP protocal with Bob Kahn.Cerf began his work at the United States Department of Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA).He played a huge role in leading the development of the Internet and Internet data packet and security technologies. Cerf helped fund and form Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN). Cerf has received numerous notable awards such as IEE Alexander Graham Bell Medal(1997), Marconi Prize(1998), Presidential Medal of Freedom(2005), and more recently ForMemRS(2016).

J.C.R. Licklider

Joseph Carl Robnett Licklider also known as J.C.R Licklider(March 11, 1915-June 26, 1990) was an American psychologist and computer scientist. He is remembered for being one of the first foresee modern-style interactive computing. He has been called "computing's Johnny Appleseed", for planting the seeds of computing in the digital age. He brought the first production of Programmed Data Processor-1(PDP-1) computer and conducted the first public demonstration of time-sharing. In 1963 he was the Director of Behavioral Sciences Command and Control Research at ARPA. He wanted to establish a time-sharing network of computer with the software of the era. His vision ultimately led to ARPANet, which is the predecessor of today's Internet.
